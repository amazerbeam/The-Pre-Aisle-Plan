# Chef — Cuisine notes

Compact reference for the cuisines the FoodBytes chef most commonly draws from. Use as a starting point — when designing a less common dish, also `WebSearch` for the authentic technique ("X traditional recipe from scratch", "X authentic [cuisine] technique").

## Ingredient standards (all cuisines)

- **Preferred fats:** butter, ghee, olive oil, coconut oil, lard, tallow, duck fat, avocado oil, schmaltz.
- **High-heat exceptions:** cold-pressed sunflower or canola when olive oil's smoke point is too low (deep fry, very high stir-fry).
- **Banned:** hydrogenated/partially-hydrogenated oils, margarine with trans fats, unspecified "vegetable oil" blends.
- **Sweeteners:** honey, maple, coconut sugar, cane sugar, molasses, dates.
- **Asian specialty items:** Asia Market (asiamarket.ie). Everyday: Tesco Ireland.

## Venezuelan

Key dishes: **Arepas** (corn flatbreads with reina pepiada / pabellón / queso), **Pabellón Criollo** (carne mechada + caraotas negras + rice + tajadas), **Cachapas** (sweet corn pancakes with queso de mano), **Tequeños** (fried cheese sticks in wheat dough), **Hallacas** (corn-dough tamales, Christmas), **Asado Negro** (beef in papelón sauce), **Hervido** (root-veg soup), **Patacones/Tostones** (twice-fried green plantain), **Tajadas** (fried ripe plantain), **Caraotas Negras** (black beans with sofrito + papelón).

Essential ingredients (Tesco alt): Harina P.A.N. (world foods aisle / online), plátano (Tesco stocks), queso blanco (halloumi/feta sub), papelón (dark muscovado), ají dulce (mini sweet peppers), culantro (fresh coriander), comino (ground cumin).

Techniques: **sofrito** base (onion / garlic / pepper / tomato); **arepas** — harina P.A.N. + warm salted water, rest 5 min, griddle 5–7 min per side; **carne mechada** — slow-cook flank/brisket then shred and sauté with sofrito; **caraotas** — soak overnight, slow cook with bay leaf, finish with sofrito + papelón.

## Spanish

Key dishes: **Paella** (saffron rice — chase the socarrat), **Tortilla Española** (thick potato-onion omelette), **Gambas al Ajillo**, **Patatas Bravas**, **Croquetas** (béchamel + jamón/chicken/cod), **Gazpacho**, **Pulpo a la Gallega**, **Pimientos de Padrón**, **Chorizo a la Sidra**, **Fabada Asturiana**, **Albóndigas**, **Pisto**, **Churros con Chocolate**.

Essentials (Tesco alt): pimentón / smoked paprika (Tesco), saffron (spice aisle), cooking chorizo (deli), jamón serrano (deli), morcilla (specialty), manchego (Tesco), sherry vinegar, bomba/calasparra rice (arborio sub), piquillo peppers (jarred world foods), extra-virgin olive oil generously.

Techniques: **sofrito español** — onion/garlic/tomato cooked slowly to jammy; **paella** — never stir once stock is in, high heat at the end for socarrat; **tortilla flip** — slide onto plate, invert back; **gambas al ajillo** — oil hot enough to sizzle garlic instantly, serve still bubbling; **croquetas** — very thick béchamel chilled fully before shaping, double-bread; **al pil-pil** — emulsify EVOO with fish gelatin by swirling the pan.

## Other cuisines (Italian, Chinese, Thai, Indian, French, Japanese, Mexican)

Standard techniques apply; key reminders:

- **Italian** — pasta water is a sauce ingredient; finish pasta in the sauce, not on the plate.
- **Chinese** — wok hei needs real heat and a hot dry wok; cornstarch slurry at the end thickens to gloss.
- **Thai** — balance salty / sweet / sour / spicy in every dish; fish sauce, palm sugar, lime, chilli.
- **Indian** — bloom whole spices in fat first, then ground spices; tomato/onion masala is the base.
- **French** — mirepoix and proper deglazing; mounting butter (monter au beurre) for sauce shine.
- **Japanese** — dashi as the umami floor; light hand with seasoning, let the produce/fish lead.
- **Mexican** — char tomatoes/onions/chillis on a dry comal before blending salsas; toast tortillas, don't fry into chips for tacos.

## When to spawn a sub-recipe

Default to homemade for any of: bread, pita, tortilla, wrap, pizza dough, pasta, pesto, dressing, marinade, stock, spice blend, sauce that exists across multiple dishes. See `.claude/rules/homemade-first-and-ingredient-dedup.md`.
