# Market Selection Guide

## Market Rankings by Edge Opportunity

### Tier 1: Best for Finding Value
| Market | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Match Result (1X2)** | Most liquid, most data, easiest to analyze |
| **Both Teams to Score (BTTS)** | Defensive/offensive form creates mispricings |
| **Over/Under 2.5 Goals** | Public bias toward overs; unders often valuable |

### Tier 2: Decent Opportunities
| Market | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Double Chance** | Lower odds but safer; good for protecting value |
| **Draw No Bet** | Insurance against the most unpredictable outcome |
| **Asian Handicap** | Reduces variance; good for slight favorites |

### Tier 3: Avoid for Profit
| Market | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Accumulators** | House edge compounds; 4-fold = ~15%+ margin |
| **Correct Score** | Too many outcomes; impossible to estimate accurately |
| **First Goalscorer** | High variance, high margin |
| **Specials/Props** | Entertainment bets; margins are huge |

## When to Use Each Market

### Match Result (1X2)
- Default choice when you have opinion on winner
- Best when one team significantly undervalued
- Skip when match is genuinely 50/50

### BTTS Yes/No
- Use when teams have clear attacking/defensive identity
- BTTS No valuable when facing parked bus
- BTTS Yes valuable when both teams need a win

### Over/Under 2.5 Goals
- Under valuable in dead rubbers, mid-table clashes
- Over valuable when top teams face leaky defenses
- Check team's O/U record for season

## Combining Markets (If Required)

If your group requires accumulators, minimize damage:
- Maximum 2-3 selections
- Use only Tier 1 markets
- All selections should have individual value
- Accept this is -EV but limits losses vs larger accas
