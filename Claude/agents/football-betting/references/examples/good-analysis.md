# Good Analysis Example

## Match: Nottingham Forest vs Brentford

### Research Done
- Forest: W-W-D-L-W last 5 home (strong at City Ground)
- Brentford: L-D-L-W-L last 5 away (poor travelers)
- H2H: Forest won last 2 at home
- Injuries: Brentford missing Toney (key striker)
- Context: Mid-table clash, no pressure on either side

### Odds Analysis
```
Forest Win: 2.40 (implied 41.7%)
Draw: 3.30 (implied 30.3%)
Brentford Win: 3.00 (implied 33.3%)
```

### My Estimates
- Forest Win: 48% (home form + H2H + Toney absence)
- Draw: 28%
- Brentford Win: 24%

### Value Calculation
```
Forest: 48% - 41.7% = +6.3% edge ✅
Draw: 28% - 30.3% = -2.3% no value ❌
Brentford: 24% - 33.3% = -9.3% no value ❌
```

### Recommendation
**Pick:** Nottingham Forest to Win
**Odds:** 2.40
**Edge:** +6.3%
**Stake:** 3% of bankroll (€1.50 from €50)

**Why:** Forest's home form is significantly better than their overall record suggests. Brentford struggle away and are without their main goal threat. The bookies are pricing this as a coin flip, but Forest are closer to slight favorites at home.

**Risk:** Forest can be inconsistent. If Brentford score first, Forest may struggle to break them down.

---

## What Makes This Good

1. ✅ Researched both teams' recent form
2. ✅ Checked home/away splits (not just overall)
3. ✅ Noted key injury
4. ✅ Converted odds to probability
5. ✅ Estimated own probability with reasoning
6. ✅ Calculated edge before recommending
7. ✅ Sized stake appropriately for edge
8. ✅ Acknowledged what could go wrong
