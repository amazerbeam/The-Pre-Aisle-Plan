# Bad Analysis Examples

## Mistake 1: Betting Without Research

❌ **Bad:**
> "Manchester United are a big club, they should beat Bournemouth at home. Bet €10 on United."

**Why it's bad:**
- No recent form check
- "Big club" isn't analysis
- No odds evaluation
- Oversized stake with no reasoning

✅ **Better:** Check United's current form, Bournemouth's away record, compare to odds offered.

---

## Mistake 2: Ignoring Odds

❌ **Bad:**
> "Liverpool will definitely beat Luton, they're way better. Bet on Liverpool."

**Why it's bad:**
- Liverpool probably WILL win, but at 1.15 odds (87% implied), they need to win 87%+ of the time to be profitable
- "Better team" ≠ value bet
- Heavy favorites are often bad bets

✅ **Better:** If Liverpool win 85% of such games but odds imply 87%, there's NEGATIVE value. Skip it.

---

## Mistake 3: Accumulators for Profit

❌ **Bad:**
> "I've got a 5-fold acca: Arsenal, City, Liverpool, Chelsea, Spurs all to win. Combined odds 8.50!"

**Why it's bad:**
- Each leg has ~5% bookmaker margin
- 5 legs compounds to ~25%+ margin against you
- One upset kills entire bet
- Fun? Yes. Profitable? No.

✅ **Better:** If you must acca, keep to 2-3 legs max, only selections with individual value.

---

## Mistake 4: Chasing Losses

❌ **Bad:**
> "Lost €10 yesterday, putting €20 on this to get it back."

**Why it's bad:**
- Yesterday's loss doesn't change today's probabilities
- Doubling stakes after losses = bankruptcy guaranteed
- Emotional betting destroys bankrolls

✅ **Better:** Stick to stake sizing rules regardless of recent results.

---

## Mistake 5: Narrative Over Numbers

❌ **Bad:**
> "This is a derby match, anything can happen! I'll bet on the underdog because they'll be fired up!"

**Why it's bad:**
- "Anything can happen" isn't analysis
- "Fired up" is unquantifiable
- No comparison to odds
- Underdog may still be bad value

✅ **Better:** Derbies CAN create value (home crowds lift underdogs), but still need to check if odds already price this in.

---

## Mistake 6: Certainty Language

❌ **Bad:**
> "Arsenal are GUARANTEED to win this. Easy money. Safe bet."

**Why it's bad:**
- Nothing in football is guaranteed
- "Safe bet" doesn't exist
- Overconfidence leads to oversized stakes
- This language hides uncertainty

✅ **Better:** Always frame as probabilities, acknowledge risks.
