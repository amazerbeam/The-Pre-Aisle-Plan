# Variant System

How to create recipe families with different portion sizes.

## Core Principle

**The same recipe works for weight loss AND weight gain. Only portion sizes change.**

Variants are NOT about removing or adding ingredients — they're about scaling portions.

## The Three Variants

| Variant | Portion Size | Target Calories | Use Case |
|---------|--------------|-----------------|----------|
| **Light** | Smaller portions | ~450-550 cal | Weight loss, big earlier meal |
| **Standard** | Normal portions | ~550-650 cal | Maintenance (DEFAULT) |
| **Full** | Larger portions | ~700-800 cal | Weight gain, active days |

## How Portions Scale

**All components scale together** (except vegetables, which stay generous).

### Example: Chicken & Rice

| Component | Light | Standard | Full |
|-----------|-------|----------|------|
| Chicken breast | 120g | 150g | 200g |
| Rice (cooked) | ¾ cup | 1 cup | 1.5 cups |
| Vegetables | 1 cup | 1 cup | 1 cup |
| Cooking oil | ½ tbsp | 1 tbsp | 1 tbsp |
| **Total Calories** | ~500 cal | ~650 cal | ~800 cal |
| **Protein** | ~40g | ~50g | ~65g |

### Example: Salmon & Potato

| Component | Light | Standard | Full |
|-----------|-------|----------|------|
| Salmon fillet | 120g | 150g | 200g |
| Potato | Small | Medium | Large |
| Butter | ½ tbsp | 1 tbsp | 1.5 tbsp |
| Vegetables | 1.5 cups | 1.5 cups | 1.5 cups |
| **Total Calories** | ~480 cal | ~620 cal | ~780 cal |

## Creating Variants

### Step 1: Design Standard First
- Balanced meal with normal portions
- ~150g protein per person
- Reasonable fats
- Mark as DEFAULT

### Step 2: Create Light
Scale DOWN all components (except vegetables):
- Protein: ~80% of Standard
- Carbs: ~75% of Standard
- Fats: ~50-75% of Standard
- Vegetables: Keep the same or increase

### Step 3: Create Full
Scale UP all components:
- Protein: ~130% of Standard
- Carbs: ~150% of Standard
- Fats: ~100-125% of Standard
- Vegetables: Keep the same

### Step 4: Verify Calorie Gaps

| Check | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| Light to Standard gap | ≥100 cal difference |
| Standard to Full gap | ≥100 cal difference |
| All variants | Provide accurate macros |

## Variant Summary Table

Present all variants together:

```markdown
## [Recipe Name] Family

| Variant | Cal | Protein | Fat | Carbs | Portions |
|---------|-----|---------|-----|-------|----------|
| Light | 500 | 40g | 18g | 45g | 120g chicken, ¾ cup rice |
| Standard | 650 | 50g | 22g | 60g | 150g chicken, 1 cup rice |
| Full | 800 | 65g | 28g | 75g | 200g chicken, 1.5 cups rice |
```

## What NOT To Do

| Wrong Approach | Why It's Wrong |
|----------------|----------------|
| Light = Remove carbs entirely | User still wants rice, just less of it |
| Light = Vegetarian version | Protein drops too much |
| Light = Different recipe | Defeats the purpose — user chose THIS dish |
| Full = Add extra ingredients | Keep it the same dish, just more of it |

## The User's Choice

The Chef provides accurate macro info for each variant. The USER decides which variant fits their day:

- Had a big breakfast? → Pick Light for dinner
- Training day? → Pick Full
- Normal day? → Pick Standard

**The chef makes good food. The user picks their portion.**
