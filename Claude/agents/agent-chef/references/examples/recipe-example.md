## Thai Green Curry

**Cuisine:** Thai | **Time:** 15 min prep + 25 min cook | **Servings:** 2

### Why This Works
High heat sears the chicken for texture while lite coconut milk keeps fat in check. Building curry paste in the pan creates depth without jarred sauces.

### Ingredients

#### For the Curry
- [ ] Chicken breast (400g), sliced
- [ ] Lite coconut milk (400ml)
- [ ] Green curry paste (3 tbsp, from scratch or Biona)
- [ ] Chicken stock (100ml)
- [ ] Fish sauce alternative: Worcestershire sauce (1 tbsp)
- [ ] Brown sugar (1 tsp)
- [ ] Thai basil alternative: Fresh basil (handful)
- [ ] Lime (1, juiced)
- [ ] Coconut oil (1 tbsp)

#### Vegetables
- [ ] Bamboo shoots (100g)
- [ ] Baby corn (100g)
- [ ] Mangetout (100g)

### Instructions

1. **Sear chicken** — Heat coconut oil in wok over high heat. Add chicken, cook 3-4 min until golden. Remove, set aside.

2. **Build curry base** — Same wok, add curry paste. Fry 1 min until fragrant (smells floral, not raw).

3. **Add liquid** — Pour in coconut milk and stock. Stir to combine. Simmer 5 min.

4. **Cook vegetables** — Add bamboo, corn, mangetout. Cook 5 min until just tender.

5. **Finish** — Return chicken. Add Worcestershire, sugar, lime juice. Simmer 3 min. Stir in basil.

### Chef's Notes
- **Make Ahead:** Curry paste keeps 1 week refrigerated
- **Storage:** Refrigerate 3 days, freezes well
- **Swap:** Prawns work well, add last 3 minutes only
